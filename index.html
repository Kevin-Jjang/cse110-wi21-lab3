Poetry

The fuzzy powder sprinkles all over me Like the spicy cinnamon flaring my nostrils So I avoid the cool sharpness of that tickle. The glitter takes me in light dandelion fuzz And one hop shakes it all away, The way that water jumps and splatters All around the slippery poolside. Those fluttering doilies twinkle in symmetry, Layering the white patio for us In time for the sweater seasons.
